About Me

Hi there, welcome to my website! Currently (2024), I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the School of Information and Library Science (SILS), UNC-Chapel Hill.

I study how individuals’ contexts influence their interaction with systems, as well as how contexts influence their preferences for music. This short paper shows my conceptualization of individuals’ contexts and establishes the foundational framework for my dissertation study.

My dissertation explores how individuals’ preference for music diversity fluctuate within various contexts.

In my previous research experiences, I’ve applied a wide array of skills across many user studies. These skills including survey, interview, log analysis, data visualization, and qualitative coding. Additionally, I have a solid understanding of user behavior within information systems.
